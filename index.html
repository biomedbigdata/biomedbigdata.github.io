Biography

Markus List heads the group of Big Data in Biomedicine which is part of the Chair of Experimental Bioinformatics at the Technical University of Munich. We are located in the TUM School of Life Sciences in Freising-Weihenstephan.

We develop predictive models and software tools that integrate big heterogeneous (OMICS) data. Our long term vision is that our research finds application in clinical practice. Towards this goal, we also address challenges in data privacy as well as in explainability and continuity in modelling.

Selected Publications

An ontology-based method for assessing batch effect adjustment approaches in heterogeneous datasets

We present a novel method for assessing the performance of batch effect adjustment methods on heterogeneous data. Our method borrows information from the Cell Ontology to establish if batch adjustment leads to a better agreement between observed pairwise similarity and similarity of cell types inferred from the ontology.
Florian Schmidt, Markus List, Engin Cukuroglu, Sebastian Köhler, Jonathan Göke, Marcel H Schulz
European Conference on Computational Biology (ECCB), 2018
